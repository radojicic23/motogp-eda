When considering total number of wins, Japanese manufacturers have won more than any other. When analyzing closer we can see that Japanese manufacturers wins concentrate in the MotoGP, followed by the 125cc and the 250cc. This could suggest that Japanese manufacturers are particularly good at producing large-sized 4-stroke engines and small-medium sized 2-stroke engines. When looking at Italian manufacturers wins we see that in the 125cc, closely followed by the MotoGP and 250cc categories (with very similar values). The most interesting fact here is that Italian manufacturers have won all editions of the MotoE. When looking at these numbers we can say that Japan is the most successful nation when in comes to manufacturing racing motorcycles.

Interestingly it seems that the number of drivers per country follows a power law (most of the drivers come from a few of countries).

Interestingly, the number of championships won per nation follows the same pattern as above.

HOW THE CHAMPIONSHIP WINS DISTRIBUTE OVER THE DRIVERS FOR EACH COUNTRY?

As we can see, even the championships won within a nation follows a power law. That means that a few of drivers make up for the majority for the championship wins. This also means that to a greater extent, a few drivers have won the majority of championships.

% OF DRIVERS THAT HAVE WON CHAMPIONSHIPS

This chart works based on the previous one. With this we can see that most drivers never actually win a championship, while only a small percentage of them actually goes on to win several ones.

NUMBER OF 1ST, 2ND, 3RD PLACES PER COUNTRY

From the drivers data we can see that Italy is the most winning nation when it comes to drivers, that means that Italian drivers have the most wins, there are followed by Spanish and British drivers.

CONCLUSION ON DRIVERS DATA

Interesting thing is the fact that the number of championship wins follows a power law, which means that a few of drivers win the most championships, just like a few of drivers have the most wins, 2nd places and 3rd places in races. This pattern also repeats when it comes to the wins of drivers within the same nation.

FINAL REPORT

Based on the data analyzed in this notebook, we saw that Italy is the 2nd best manufacutrer of morotcycles and the country with the best drivers. While Japan is the best when it comes to the constructor championships, it's in 8th place when it comes to championships won by its drivers. On the flip side, Spain is the 2nd country when it comes to drivers championship wins but does not have any significant constructor championship wins. Since Italy is the only nation that has very good number when it comes to both constructor and drivers championships, it's reasonable to say that overall Italy is the best nation in MotoGP.
